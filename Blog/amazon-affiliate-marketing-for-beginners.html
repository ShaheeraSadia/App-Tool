Amazon Affiliate Marketing for Beginners: Complete Step-by-Step Guide (2026)

Shaheera Sadia June 2026 15 min read 52 Comments
Amazon Affiliate Marketing for Beginners
What it is · How it works · How to earn your first commission

You don't need a big audience, an expensive website, or any special skills to start earning money with Amazon's affiliate program. In this guide, you'll learn exactly how it works, how to sign up, how to get your first affiliate link, and how to actually earn commissions — all explained in plain, simple language.

Amazon's affiliate program — called Amazon Associates — is one of the most popular ways people earn money online. Millions of bloggers, YouTubers, social media creators, and website owners use it every single day. And the best part? It's completely free to join.

The basic idea is simple: you share a special link to a product on Amazon. When someone clicks your link and buys that product, you earn a small percentage of the sale price. Amazon handles everything else — payments, shipping, customer service. You just share the link.

What Is Amazon Affiliate Marketing?

Amazon affiliate marketing means you promote Amazon products using special tracking links. When someone buys through your link, Amazon pays you a commission — usually between 1% and 10% of the sale price, depending on the product category.

Here's a very simple example:

  • You write a blog post about "the best headphones for working from home"
  • You include an Amazon affiliate link to a pair of headphones that costs $80
  • A reader clicks your link and buys those headphones
  • Amazon pays you about 3% commission = $2.40 for that one sale

That sounds small. But if 100 people a day click your links and 5% of them buy something, you're making $120+ per day from a blog post you wrote once. That's the power of passive income.

Good to Know When someone clicks your Amazon affiliate link, Amazon tracks them for 24 hours. If they buy anything on Amazon during that 24-hour window — not just the product you linked to — you earn a commission on the entire order.

✅ Pros of Amazon Associates

  • Free to join — no cost ever
  • Millions of products to promote
  • People already trust Amazon
  • You earn on the whole cart, not just your product
  • Amazon handles shipping, returns, customer support
  • Works for any niche or topic
  • Beginner-friendly dashboard

Cons to Know

  • Commission rates are low (1–10%)
  • Only 24-hour cookie window
  • Must make 3 sales in 180 days or account closes
  • Minimum payout is $10 (gift card) or $100 (bank transfer)
  • Cannot use affiliate links in email newsletters
  • Some categories pay very low (1–2%)

Step 1 — How to Sign Up for Amazon Associates

1

Go to affiliate-program.amazon.com

Open your browser and go to affiliate-program.amazon.com. Click the orange "Sign Up" button. You'll need an existing Amazon account — the same one you use to shop on Amazon. If you don't have one, create a free Amazon account first.

Use your real name and real information. Amazon reviews all applications manually. Fake or incomplete information will get your application rejected.
2

Enter your website or social media URL

Amazon will ask for the website, blog, YouTube channel, or social media profile where you plan to share your affiliate links. You must have at least one active website or social media account to apply. Enter your URL clearly.

If you have a blog — even a small one with just a few posts — that's enough. If you have a YouTube channel, Instagram, or Pinterest account with some content, that works too. Amazon just needs to see that you have a real platform where you'll share links.

Don't apply with an empty website or a brand new social media account with zero posts. Build at least 5–10 pieces of content first, then apply.
3

Choose your Associate Store ID

This is your unique ID that Amazon uses to track your sales. It usually looks like your website name followed by a number, for example: toolkitpro-20. This ID is embedded in every affiliate link you create. Choose something simple and related to your brand.

4

Describe what your website is about

Amazon will ask a few questions about your website: what topics you cover, how you drive traffic, and how you plan to use affiliate links. Be honest and specific. Write clearly — for example: "I run a blog about image optimization and web tools. I publish how-to guides and tool reviews for bloggers and web developers."

5

Enter your payment and tax information

You'll need to enter your bank account details or choose Amazon gift cards as your payment method. You'll also need to fill in a tax form (a simple W-9 for US residents, or a W-8BEN for international affiliates). This is required before Amazon will pay you any commissions.

International applicants (outside the US) can still join Amazon Associates. You can receive payment via Amazon gift cards (min $10), bank transfer (min $100), or cheque (min $100).
6

Get approved and make 3 sales in 180 days

Amazon gives you a provisional approval immediately, but you must make at least 3 qualifying sales within 180 days for your account to stay active. Amazon then manually reviews your account. If you don't make 3 sales, your account is closed — but you can reapply.

This is why it's important to start sharing your links right away, not wait until your site is "perfect."

Step 2 — How to Get Your Affiliate Links

Once approved, getting affiliate links is quick and easy. There are two main ways:

Method 1: SiteStripe Toolbar (Easiest)

When you're logged into your Amazon Associates account and you visit any Amazon product page, a special toolbar appears at the top of the page. This is called the SiteStripe. It lets you create affiliate links for any product in seconds.

Just click "Text" in the SiteStripe bar and Amazon instantly gives you a short, clean affiliate link you can copy and paste anywhere. No need to log into your dashboard — it works right on the product page.

Method 2: Product Links Tool in Your Dashboard

Log into your Associates Central dashboard at associates.amazon.com. Go to Product Links → Product Links. Search for any product by keyword or paste a product URL. Click "Get Link" and copy the code. You can choose between a text link, an image link, or a combination of both.

Important Rule You must always disclose that your links are affiliate links. You are legally required (by FTC rules in the US, and similar laws in other countries) and required by Amazon's own rules to clearly state near your affiliate links: "As an Amazon Associate, I earn from qualifying purchases." Never hide this disclosure.

Step 3 — Where to Share Your Affiliate Links

This is the most important part — you can have the best affiliate links in the world, but if no one clicks them, you earn nothing. Here are the best places to share your Amazon affiliate links:

1. Blog / Website (Best for Long-Term Income)

A blog is the most powerful platform for affiliate marketing. Write helpful articles that people search for on Google — like "best laptop for college students" or "top kitchen tools under $50." Include your affiliate links naturally within the content. Once your blog post ranks on Google, it sends traffic and earns commissions 24/7 without you doing anything extra.

2. YouTube Videos

YouTube is the second-biggest search engine in the world. Make videos reviewing products, comparing items, or showing how to use something. Put your affiliate links in the video description. Every time someone watches your video and clicks a link, you have a chance to earn.

3. Pinterest

Pinterest is excellent for affiliate marketing — especially for home decor, fashion, food, beauty, crafts, and lifestyle products. Create pins that link directly to your blog posts or product pages with your affiliate links. Pinterest users are in "buying mode" — they're actively looking for ideas and products to purchase.

4. Instagram and TikTok

Social media platforms work well for product recommendations, unboxings, and reviews. Put your affiliate link in your bio (Instagram) or use the link-in-bio tool (TikTok). When you post about a product, direct followers to your bio link. These platforms are visual, so they work especially well for beauty, fashion, fitness, and tech products.

5. Facebook Groups and Pages

If you manage a Facebook page or group around a specific topic (gardening, cooking, home improvement, etc.), you can share product recommendations with affiliate links. Be helpful first — share tips and value — and include product links naturally. Don't spam links or you'll lose followers fast.

Where You CANNOT Share Amazon Affiliate Links Amazon's rules explicitly ban affiliate links in email newsletters, PDF downloads, eBooks, or offline materials. You can only use them on websites, social media, and apps. Breaking this rule can get your account permanently banned.

Step 4 — How to Pick the Right Products to Promote

Not every product is worth promoting. Choosing the right products is one of the most important skills in affiliate marketing. Here's how to think about it:

Promote Products You Actually Know or Use

The most convincing recommendations come from personal experience. If you own and love a product, you can write or talk about it with genuine enthusiasm and specific details. Readers and viewers trust this much more than a generic "this product is great" recommendation for something you've never touched.

Focus on Products With Good Reviews

Look for products with at least 4+ stars and 100+ reviews on Amazon. These products sell well because customers trust them. If you send someone to a product with 20 bad reviews, they won't buy it — and you earn nothing. High-rated products convert visitors into buyers much more reliably.

Consider the Price Point

There's a sweet spot for product prices: between $30 and $200. Products under $10 earn tiny commissions ($0.10–$0.50 per sale) — not worth the effort. Products over $500 are purchased rarely because people think carefully before spending that much. Products in the $30–$200 range are affordable enough to buy impulsively, but expensive enough to earn a meaningful commission.

Check If It's Frequently Purchased Together

Amazon shows "Frequently Bought Together" sections on product pages. If you promote one product, often the buyer will add the accessories or related items to their cart too — and you earn commission on those as well. This significantly increases your earnings per click.

Step 5 — Content Ideas That Earn Commissions

The type of content you create has a huge impact on how much you earn. Some content types convert visitors into buyers much better than others. Here are the best ones:

"Best [Product] for [Use Case]" Lists

These are the most powerful affiliate content pieces. Examples: "Best Cameras for Travel Photography," "Best Laptops for Students Under $600," "Best Kitchen Tools for Beginners." These articles match exactly what people type into Google when they're ready to buy. They already know they want the product category — they just need help choosing which one.

Product Reviews

Write a detailed, honest review of a specific product. Cover what's good, what's not so good, who it's best for, and who should skip it. Add your affiliate link at the bottom with a clear "Check current price on Amazon" button. Reviews work because they target people who are already interested in that specific product and just need one more push.

"[Product A] vs [Product B]" Comparisons

Comparison articles work incredibly well. "iPhone vs Samsung," "Kindle vs iPad for Reading," "Standing Desk vs Regular Desk." People searching for comparisons are almost always about to make a purchase — they just can't decide which one to buy. Include affiliate links for both products and earn no matter which one they choose.

"How to" Guides With Product Recommendations

Tutorial content can include natural product recommendations. "How to Start a YouTube Channel" → recommend cameras, microphones, ring lights. "How to Set Up a Home Office" → recommend chairs, desks, monitors, webcams. The recommendations feel helpful rather than salesy because they're embedded in genuinely useful content.

"Gift Guide" Articles

Gift guides are evergreen money-makers, especially during holiday seasons. "Best Gifts for Photographers," "Christmas Gifts for Teens Under $50," "Mother's Day Gifts She'll Actually Use." These articles get massive search traffic in November and December and can earn hundreds of dollars per month from affiliate links during peak shopping season.

Important Amazon Associates Rules to Follow

Amazon has strict rules. Breaking them can get your account permanently banned — and you'll lose all unpaid commissions. Here are the most important rules every beginner needs to know:

  • Always disclose affiliate links. You must clearly state you earn from qualifying purchases. Add a short disclosure at the top of every page or post that has affiliate links: "This post contains Amazon affiliate links. I earn a small commission if you buy through my links at no extra cost to you."
  • Never share links in emails. Sending affiliate links via email (newsletters, auto-responders, etc.) violates Amazon's Terms of Service. Links must only be shared on websites, social media, and apps.
  • Never use Amazon images without permission. Don't download and re-upload Amazon product photos. Use Amazon's official Product Advertising API to display images, or use Amazon's official widgets. Using their images directly violates their image policy.
  • Never share shortened/masked affiliate links. Link shorteners like bit.ly that hide the fact it's an Amazon link are against Amazon's rules. Use the full Amazon link or Amazon's official short link format (amzn.to).
  • Never click your own affiliate links. Clicking your own links to test them or to generate commissions is fraud. Amazon will detect this and ban your account immediately.
  • Don't use affiliate links on sites with prohibited content. Sites with adult content, violence, or illegal content are not allowed in the program.
  • Keep your pricing information updated. Never quote specific prices in your content (prices change on Amazon). Instead, always write "Check current price on Amazon" and link to the product page.

How and When Does Amazon Pay You?

Amazon pays affiliate commissions approximately 60 days after the end of the month in which the commission was earned. This means there's always a 2-month delay between earning and receiving payment.

For example: commissions earned in January are paid at the end of March.

10 Tips to Earn More With Amazon Affiliates

  1. Target buyer-intent keywords. Write content for people who are ready to buy, not just researching. "Best laptops under $500" converts far better than "what is a laptop."
  2. Add "Buy on Amazon" buttons. Styled, prominent buttons with clear calls to action (like "Check Price on Amazon →") get far more clicks than plain text links.
  3. Link early in your content. Don't hide your affiliate links at the very bottom. Put the first link near the top of the page so readers who are ready to buy don't have to scroll.
  4. Update old content. Go back to your older posts and add affiliate links. Older content often gets more traffic because it's had time to rank on Google.
  5. Write for holiday seasons. Create gift guides and seasonal content well before Christmas, Mother's Day, Black Friday, and Prime Day. These events generate massive affiliate income.
  6. Focus on high-traffic, low-competition topics. Use free keyword research tools like Google's free Keyword Planner or AnswerThePublic to find topics people search for that don't have many results already.
  7. Use product comparison tables. Tables that compare 3–5 products side by side are highly effective. They help readers make decisions quickly — and each product in the table has its own affiliate link.
  8. Build email list — just don't put links in emails. You can use email to drive traffic to your blog posts, where the affiliate links live. This is allowed and very effective.
  9. Track your performance. Use Amazon's tracking IDs to see which pages, posts, or videos are generating the most clicks and sales. Double down on what's working.
  10. Be patient. Most affiliate marketers don't earn significant income in their first 3 months. It takes time to build traffic. Keep publishing, keep improving, and the commissions will follow.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I join Amazon Associates from Pakistan, India, or other countries?
Yes! Amazon Associates is available in most countries. Affiliates in Pakistan, India, Bangladesh, and most other countries can join. You'll receive payments via Amazon gift cards (minimum $10) or international bank transfer (minimum $100). You'll also need to complete a W-8BEN tax form for international tax purposes — Amazon walks you through it during signup.
Do I need a website to join Amazon Associates?
You need at least one active platform — this can be a website, blog, YouTube channel, Instagram account, Facebook page, TikTok account, or mobile app. Amazon just needs to see that you have a real audience to share products with. A simple blog with 5–10 helpful articles is enough to apply with.
How long does Amazon Associates approval take?
You get provisional approval instantly when you sign up. However, Amazon manually reviews your account after you make your first 3 qualifying sales. This review usually takes 1–3 business days. During the 180-day provisional period, your account is fully functional and you can earn commissions — the review just confirms your account stays permanently open.
What happens if I don't make 3 sales in 180 days?
Amazon will close your account. However, you can simply re-apply. There is no penalty for reapplying. Many beginners get rejected or closed on their first try and successfully reopen their account after building more content and traffic. Any commissions already earned before closure are still paid to you.
Can I use Amazon affiliate links on Pinterest?
Yes — Amazon affiliate links are allowed on Pinterest. You can pin product images with your affiliate link or create pins that link to your blog posts containing affiliate links. Pinterest is actually one of the best platforms for Amazon affiliate marketing, especially for lifestyle, home, fashion, and beauty products. Make sure your pin descriptions include a clear affiliate disclosure.
How much traffic do I need to make money with Amazon affiliates?
A rough rule of thumb: for every 1,000 page visitors, you might earn $5–$20 in Amazon affiliate commissions — depending on your niche, your content quality, and what products you promote. At 10,000 visitors per month in a decent niche, many affiliates earn $50–$200/month from Amazon alone. High-ticket niches (electronics, cameras, tools) earn significantly more per click.
