Best Image Sizes for Every Social Media Platform in 2026 — Complete Guide

Shaheera Sadia May 14, 2026 10 min read 6,872 views

Have you ever uploaded a photo to Instagram and found it was weirdly cropped? Or posted something to Pinterest and it looked tiny compared to everyone else's pins? Or put an image on Facebook only to see it come out blurry?

This happens all the time — and it is almost always caused by uploading an image at the wrong size. Every social media platform has its own rules about how images should be sized. When your image matches those rules, it looks sharp, clear, and full-size. When it does not match, the platform stretches, shrinks, or crops your photo — and it ends up looking bad.

This guide gives you the exact image dimensions for every major social media platform in 2026 — in plain, simple language. Bookmark this page and refer back to it every time you create content.

Quick summary: Every platform prefers vertical images (taller than wide) for feed posts because most people browse on mobile phones. Use the exact pixel sizes in this guide and your images will always look perfect — no cropping, no blurriness, no white bars.

Why Getting the Right Image Size Matters

You might be thinking — does it really matter if my image is a slightly different size? The answer is yes, and here is why:

  • Wrong size = blurry image. If your image is smaller than what the platform expects, it has to stretch it to fill the space. Stretching makes photos look blurry and low quality. This makes your content look unprofessional.
  • Wrong size = cropped image. If your image is the wrong shape, the platform crops off parts of it — sometimes cutting off people's heads, key text, or important design elements.
  • Wrong size = less reach. On Pinterest especially, image size directly affects how much space your pin takes up in the feed. A vertically sized pin takes up more space and gets more views than a square pin of the same content.
  • Wrong size = slow loading. If you upload a 10MB photo where a 200KB photo would do, your content loads slowly on people's phones. Slow-loading images get skipped.

Spending just a few extra seconds to resize your image before uploading makes a real difference to how your content looks and performs. Now let us go through each platform one by one.

Pinterest Image Sizes 2026

Pinterest

The most image-sensitive platform — size directly affects how many people see your pins

Pinterest is different from every other social media platform because it is a visual search engine. When people scroll Pinterest on their phone, they see a grid of vertical images. The taller your image is, the more screen space it takes up — and the more space it takes up, the more people notice it.

This is why Pinterest strongly recommends vertical images. A pin that is twice as tall as it is wide will get significantly more impressions than a square pin showing the same content.

Instagram

Use portrait format for feed posts — it takes up more screen on mobile and gets more engagement

Instagram is used almost entirely on mobile phones. When someone scrolls their Instagram feed, they see one image at a time filling the screen. A portrait (vertical) image fills more of the screen than a square image — which means more visual impact and typically more engagement.

This is totally normal. Your feed engagement will be much higher with portrait images even though the grid looks cropped.

Common Image Size Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

  • Uploading a horizontal image to Pinterest — This is the most common Pinterest mistake. Horizontal images look tiny in the Pinterest feed. Always use 2:3 vertical ratio for standard pins.
  • Using the same image size for all platforms — Each platform is different. A perfect Instagram image (1080×1350) would be badly cropped on YouTube thumbnails (1280×720). Resize for each platform.
  • Uploading raw high-resolution photos (4K, 8K) — Huge files slow down your uploads and get re-compressed aggressively by platforms. There is no benefit to uploading a 20MB raw photo — compress it first.
  • Forgetting to check the safe zone on Stories — Stories on all platforms (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok) show buttons and icons overlaying the top and bottom of the image. Keep all important text and faces in the central 60% of the image height to avoid them being covered.
  • Not defining image dimensions in HTML — If you embed images on your website, always add width and height attributes. This prevents layout shift and improves your Core Web Vitals score.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does image size really affect how many people see my content?
Yes — especially on Pinterest. A 2:3 vertical pin (1000×1500px) takes up significantly more space in the Pinterest mobile feed than a square pin of the same content. More visual space means more people notice it as they scroll. On Instagram, a 4:5 portrait image takes up about 20% more screen space than a square image. That extra space directly increases how many people pause on your post.
What happens if I upload an image that is the wrong size?
Different platforms handle wrong-sized images in different ways. Most will either crop the image (cut off parts you wanted to show), stretch it (making it look blurry), or add black bars on the sides (making it look unfinished). None of these outcomes look professional. Pinterest, for example, will display your image at whatever aspect ratio you upload — but if it is horizontal, it will look tiny next to all the vertical pins surrounding it in the feed.
What is the best image format to use for social media?
JPEG is the safest and most universally accepted format across all social media platforms. WebP is even better if the platform accepts it — it gives 30% smaller file sizes at the same quality. PNG is fine for logos and graphics with transparent backgrounds, but avoid using PNG for photos as the files are unnecessarily large. For Pinterest specifically, JPEG at 85% quality or WebP at 80% quality both perform excellently.
How large should my image file size be before uploading?
For Pinterest pins: aim for under 500KB. For Instagram posts: under 1MB. For Facebook posts: under 2MB. For YouTube thumbnails: under 2MB (this is also YouTube's maximum). Staying within these ranges ensures your images upload quickly, display at good quality, and do not get excessively re-compressed by the platform. Can I use the same image for multiple platforms?
You can use the same original photo across multiple platforms, but you should resize it to the correct dimensions for each one before uploading. Do not just upload the same file everywhere — the platforms have very different aspect ratios and what looks perfect on Pinterest (2:3 portrait) will look completely different on Twitter (16:9 landscape).
