Cookie Policy

This page explains exactly what cookies Toolkit Pro uses, why we use them, and how you can control or turn them off at any time.

Last Updated: June 2026

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites save on your device (phone, tablet, or computer) when you visit them. They are very common and used by almost every website on the internet.

Cookies help websites remember things about you — like whether you've visited before, your preferences, or what's in your shopping cart. They make websites work better and allow site owners to understand how people use their sites.

Cookies cannot access other files on your device, run programs, or carry viruses. They are just small pieces of text data stored in your browser.

✅ Important Note About Toolkit Pro's Tools All tools on this website (Image Compressor, Color Extractor, QR Generator, etc.) process your files entirely inside your own browser. Your images and files are never uploaded to our servers. Cookies have nothing to do with how our tools handle your files.

Why We Use Cookies

To Keep the Site Free

Toolkit Pro is completely free to use. Every tool — Image Compressor, Color Extractor, QR Generator, Nature Designer, Art Tool, Voice Studio — is free, with no sign-up and no subscription. We keep it this way through two revenue streams:

  • Google AdSense advertising — Google shows ads on our pages. When you visit, AdSense may use cookies to show ads that are relevant to your interests. If you click an ad, we earn a small amount.
  • Amazon affiliate links — Some of our blog posts and resource pages include affiliate links. These don't use additional cookies beyond what Amazon already sets.

Without ad revenue, we would have to charge for the tools or shut down. Accepting cookies is a small way to support free tools for everyone.

To Understand How the Site Is Used

Google Analytics tells us things like: how many people visit each week, which blog posts are most popular, which tools get used most, and whether visitors come from Google, Pinterest, or direct links. This information helps us improve the site and create content people actually want.

Analytics data is completely anonymous. We never see your name, email address, or any personal information. We only see aggregate numbers — for example, "3,200 people visited the Image Compressor page this month."

Third-Party Cookies Google AdSense and Google Analytics are operated by Google LLC. They set their own cookies subject to Google's Privacy Policy. We don't control what Google does with these cookies — please review Google's policy for full details on how they handle your data.

How to Control or Turn Off Cookies

You have full control over cookies. Here are your options:

Option 1 — Use Our Cookie Banner

Every time you visit Toolkit Pro for the first time (or after clearing your browser data), a cookie consent banner appears at the bottom of the page. Click Decline to refuse advertising and analytics cookies. Only the essential cookie that remembers your choice will be saved. You can change your mind any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings" button that appears after declining.

Your Rights

If You Are in the European Union (GDPR)

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the right to:

  • Access — ask us what data we hold about you
  • Deletion — ask us to delete your data
  • Correction — ask us to correct inaccurate data
  • Restriction — ask us to limit how we use your data
  • Object — object to our use of your data for advertising
  • Portability — receive your data in a portable format

Since we collect very minimal personal data (we don't require sign-ups or accounts), most of the data that concerns you is held by Google, not by us. For data Google holds, you can manage it through your Google Account.

If You Are in California (CCPA)

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), California residents have the right to know what personal information is collected, the right to delete it, and the right to opt out of the sale of personal information. We do not sell personal information. To exercise your rights, contact us using the details below.
